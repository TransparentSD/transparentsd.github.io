# Request 25-6671

Subject: CPRA Request -- Pacific Beach Parking Program: Policy, Studies,
and Financial Records

To the Records Coordinator:

Pursuant to the California Public Records Act (Gov. Code §§ 7920.000 et
seq.), the San Diego Transparency Project requests the following public
records related to the implementation and evaluation of paid parking
meters in Pacific Beach:

Records Requested

Date Range: January 1, 2023 through present, with emphasis on decisions
and messaging around the August 2025 announcement referenced in the
Times of San Diego article:

https://timesofsandiego.com/life/2025/08/26/pacific-beach-residents-say-meters-exacerbate-parking-problems

-   Financial Planning and Budgeting

    -   Budget documents, cost estimates, and revenue forecasts for the
        Pacific Beach meter program.
    -   Any allocations or commitments for community reinvestment of
        meter revenue.
    -   Contracts, invoices, or agreements with vendors, consultants, or
        other third parties related to program planning or financial
        modeling.

-   Operational Policies

    -   Records showing how meter hours or coverage areas were
        determined, including internal memoranda or policy documents.
    -   Documents discussing the discontinuation or alignment of the
        Beach Bug shuttle program and its relationship to parking meter
        decisions.

**Processing & Timelines**

-   Please provide a determination within 10 calendar days as required
    by law.
-   If an extension is necessary, please identify the statutory basis
    under Gov. Code § 7922.535(b) and provide an estimated completion
    date.
-   If any responsive records can be produced before the entire request
    is complete, please release those portions on a rolling basis as
    they become available.

**Assistance to Clarify/Narrow**

-   If clarification would expedite processing, please contact us
    pursuant to Gov. Code § 7922.600 so we may assist in refining scope.

**Withholdings & Redactions**

-   For any withheld or redacted records, please provide:
    -   The specific statutory exemption relied upon for each.
    -   A description sufficient to identify the nature of the record
        (e.g., email chain, memo, report) and approximate volume (page
        count or number of records).
    -   An explanation of how the exemption applies, as required by Gov.
        Code § 7922.535(a).

-   Please release all reasonably segregable non-exempt portions.

**Search Scope**

-   To facilitate timely resolution, please confirm which custodians and
    systems will be searched.

    -  Likely custodians include Transportation Department (primary),
       Parking Administration, and any consultants or contractors
       involved in budgeting or financial modeling.
    -  Related custodians may include Finance staff for budget and
       revenue records, as well as the Mayor's Office and City
       Communications for policy directives or public statements tied
       to cost assumptions.


-   Please confirm if any of these related custodians will be excluded
    and provide the reason.

Thank you for your assistance and timely response.
